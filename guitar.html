BMC Guitar

A study in resonance, stability, reduced visual noise, and controlled acoustic performance.

Concept Study / In Development

Overview

This project began as an attempt to rethink the acoustic guitar as a more stable, controlled, and playable instrument. The aim was not stylistic novelty, but a change in performance behaviour through construction logic, material discipline, and reduced physical resistance.

Brief

Create an instrument that demonstrates a quieter, more deliberate approach to acoustic design — one concerned with resonance, precision, reduced ornament, and ease of interaction.

Design Intent

The project focuses on control over excess: clearer interaction, lower tension, reduced formal noise, and a closer relationship between physical object and sonic result.

Within KUON Studio

This project sits within the studio’s work on performance, examining how sound begins through touch, structure, and material behaviour. It provides context for later projects in signal, objects, and space.

What this project demonstrates

  • Acoustic thinking at instrument scale
  • Material and structural discipline
  • Reduction as a design principle
