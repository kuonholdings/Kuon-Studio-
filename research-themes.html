Research Themes

Our research explores relationships between sound, material, perception, and human experience. Themes include:

Acoustic Generation: investigating how instruments and bodies produce sound, and how resonance can be refined through construction and material logic.

Signal Transformation: examining the path from acoustic sound to electrical signal, and how transformation can be made legible through interface and form.

Listening Objects: designing objects that reproduce sound in domestic and social environments, balancing fidelity with presence and tactility.

Sound in Space: integrating sound into architectures and systems to shape atmosphere and experience.

Human Condition: understanding how sound affects rest, focus, and wellbeing, and how objects can support these conditions.
