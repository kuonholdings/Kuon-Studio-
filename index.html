MASO

Where sound becomes part of space.

MASO is an architectural system for sound, expressed through a single form — a plane — in which sound and image are held together within the same surface.

It is where Kuon Studio becomes complete in space, bringing together recording, form, and perception in a single, continuous presence.

Most architectural audio systems remove the visibility of sound, but in doing so diminish its presence. MASO does not hide sound — it gives it form.

The surface carries black ink gestures — marks through which sound was once written and organised. Within MASO, that gesture is captured, preserved, and returned as sound within the same plane.

A room designed for human perception is the optimal condition for listening. MASO works within this condition, allowing sound to exist without intrusion.

Spatial recordings are developed through dedicated capture methods, allowing the work to be preserved before it is returned as sound in space.
Sound is no longer placed into a room. It becomes part of its structure.
MASO allows sound to exist in a room without becoming equipment.

Kuon Studio emerges from work across instruments, recording, and material processes, developed between Europe and Asia.

Sound becomes real when it is given form.

MASO is the point at which this work becomes complete in space.

— Guy Traviss
London | Taiwan

STUDIO

Form is what makes it real.

Kuon Studio is concerned with how sound is given form.

This work spans from its point of origin to its presence in space — through instruments, recording, and architectural objects.

At each stage, sound is not treated as signal, but as something shaped, fixed, and experienced.
