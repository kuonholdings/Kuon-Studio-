Sound Architecture

A practice exploring the relationship between vibration, objects, and space.

Kuon Studio is a design practice founded by Guy Traviss working at the intersection of sound, objects, and architecture.

The studio explores how sound can move beyond conventional equipment and become integrated into the physical world — through instruments, architectural systems, and material structures.

Practice

Kuon Studio operates as a sound architecture practice. Rather than treating sound as an electronic product, the studio approaches it as a material that interacts with objects and space.

This perspective connects several disciplines — instrument making, acoustic engineering, product design, and architecture — into a single field of exploration.

instrument → speaker → object → architecture

Research

Kuon Studio investigates sound as a physical and cultural phenomenon.

  • Sound as architecture
  • Permanence and technological obsolescence
  • Vibration and material behaviour
  • Domestic sound environments

The studio’s ongoing research may eventually form the basis of academic work exploring the relationship between sound, architecture, and material culture.

About

Guy Traviss is a British designer working internationally at the intersection of sound, architecture, and objects.

His work spans musical instruments, loudspeaker systems, and architectural sound objects, exploring how vibration shapes spatial experience.

He is the founder of Neostein and the creator of MASO, an ongoing exploration of sound as a permanent architectural element.
