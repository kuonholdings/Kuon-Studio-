Held form — the work fixed before it is returned to space.

Sound, given structure.

Kuon Studio is a sound architecture practice concerned with how sound becomes real in the world.

Its work spans sound at origin, sound in architecture, and sound as complete form.

Discover the work →

Works

The work is structured across three conditions: origin, architecture, and completion.

01.

Guitar

Sound at origin

The guitar explores sound at its point of formation — where material, tension, and structure define how sound comes into being.

Developed in collaboration with luthier Stephen Eden, the work extends from a classical understanding of the instrument into new material and structural possibilities.

Open project →

02.

Neostein

Sound in architecture

Neostein develops architectural audio systems for integration within buildings and designed spaces.

The work explores how sound can be embedded into the fabric of architecture — through walls, surfaces, and constructed elements — balancing performance with real-world constraints.

Open project →

03.

MASO

Sound as complete form

MASO is an architectural system for sound, expressed through a single form — a plane — in which sound and image are held together within the same surface, supported by a concealed architectural volume.

It brings together recording, form, and perception in a single, continuous presence.

Profile

Guy Traviss

Kuon Studio is the practice of Guy Traviss.

Trained in music and recording, his work developed through instrument design, acoustic research, and manufacturing across Europe and China.

This experience spans the full path of sound — from its formation in instruments, through its capture in recording, to its realisation within architectural space.

Form is what makes it real.
