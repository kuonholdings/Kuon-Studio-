Sleep Speaker

A research-led study in proximity, rest, low-light interaction, and acoustic wellbeing.

Research Concept

Overview

This project examines how sound can operate in relation to rest, closeness, and the human condition. The concept treats audio not only as reproduction, but as atmosphere, comfort, and behavioural support.

Brief

Explore a bedside or near-body sound object that combines a careful acoustic presence with low-light usability, emotional softness, and a reduced physical form.

Design Intent

The project brings together sound, light, object behaviour, and sleep-adjacent ritual. It extends the studio’s work beyond performance and reproduction toward condition, care, and wellbeing.

Within KUON Studio

This project sits within the studio’s work on human experience, showing how the same design logic applied to instruments and objects can extend into rest, focus, and acoustic wellbeing.

What this project demonstrates

  • Research-led sound design
  • Human-centred object thinking
  • An expanded view of audio beyond entertainment
