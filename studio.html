Practice

KUON Studio is a design practice working across sound, objects, and spatial systems.

Its work follows sound through multiple states: from performance and physical generation, through signal and transformation, into objects, environments, and the conditions of everyday life.

This includes instruments, signal tools, listening objects, integrated systems, and research-led concepts related to rest, focus, and acoustic wellbeing.

The studio is both a design platform and a field of enquiry — a place where products, applied studies, and longer-term research can exist within the same practice.

Some projects are intended for commercial development. Others operate as prototypes, research objects, or demonstrations of a wider design position. Together they describe an approach to sound that is physical, legible, and intentional.

Profile

Guy Traviss is a designer working across sound, objects, and spatial systems.

His practice spans product development, acoustic environments, and research-led concepts, with a particular interest in how sound is generated, shaped, reproduced, and lived with.

KUON Studio serves as the public platform for this body of work, connecting instruments, signal tools, listening objects, and broader studies into the human condition.
