Philosophy

Across instruments, objects and spaces, we pursue a deliberate shaping of sonic experience. Our work is guided by a simple belief: sound should be physical, legible and intentional. We design from the origin of sound to its reproduction in space, emphasising reduction over excess, clarity over spectacle and resonance over raw output.

By stripping away superfluous features, we invite deeper engagement with material, form and behaviour. Our projects explore how technology can be quiet and tacit, how objects can change perception and how environments can support rest, focus and well‑being. We see each object as a proposition rather than a final answer – part of a broader enquiry into how sound shapes human experience.
